As cliche as it may sound, college is really where I contribute most of my maturing and figuring out who I am as a person. This most likely is because I really had to learn how to fend for myself for the first time, as I was an 8 hour drive away from home for very long periods of time. I joined an awesome organization in which I met incredible friends, and participated in a lot of University events and traditions.

I also began to learn some true programming and the theory/science behind it. This is when I really knew I made the right decision with my major and college of choice. That still holds to this day, where I still have an absolute passion for programming and can happily say I make my living doing what I love.

As of November, 2018, I have officially accepted my first full-time career position as a Software Developer for General Motors, at their IT Innovation Center in Austin, TX.
